Traveling Salesperson Problem (TSP)

Problem Statement

Given a list of destinations and the distances between each pair, what is the shortest possible route that visits each exactly once and returns to the origin?

Mathematically, we are looking for the minimum-weight Hamiltonian Cycle in a complete graph.

$$ \text{Minimize } \sum_{i=1}^{n} d(v_i, v_{i+1}) $$

Where \(v_{n+1} = v_1\) (returning to start).

The Explosion of Possibilities

TSP is one of the most famous NP-Hard problems. For just 10 houses, there are 362,880 possible routes. For 20 houses, there are 1.21 × 1017 routes—more than the seconds since the universe began!
