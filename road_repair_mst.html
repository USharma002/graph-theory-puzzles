Minimum Spanning Trees (MST)

Problem Statement

Given a connected, undirected graph \( G = (V, E) \) with edge weights \( w(e) \), find a subset of edges \( T \subseteq E \) that connects all vertices and minimizes total weight.

$$ \text{minimize } \sum_{e \in T} w(e) $$

This subset \( T \) is always a tree (no cycles) and spans the graph.

Kruskal's Algorithm

The optimal strategy (Greedy) to solve this is:

  1. Sort all edges by weight from smallest to largest.
  2. Iterate through sorted edges and add an edge only if it doesn't form a cycle with already selected edges.
  3. Stop when \( |V| - 1 \) edges are added.
