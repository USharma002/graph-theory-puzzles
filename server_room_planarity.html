Planar Graphs & Kuratowski's Theorem

What is a Planar Graph?

In graph theory, a planar graph is a graph that can be drawn on a flat 2D plane in such a way that its edges intersect only at their endpoints. In other words, it can be drawn without any edges crossing over each other.

Applications include printed circuit board (PCB) design, subway map layouts, and microchip engineering.

Kuratowski's Theorem

Polish mathematician Kazimierz Kuratowski proved that a graph is planar if and only if it does not contain a "forbidden subgraph" hidden inside it. The two forbidden shapes are:

  • \( K_5 \) : A complete graph of 5 nodes (a pentagram).
  • \( K_{3,3} \) : A bipartite graph connecting 3 items to 3 other items (like the classic "Water, Gas, Electricity" puzzle).
