The Mathematics of Blockades

Problem Statement

We model the city as a flow network \( G = (V, E) \), where road capacities are \( c(u,v) \).

Goal: Find a partition of nodes (a cut) separating the Source (\(s\)) from the Sink (\(t\)) such that the capacity of the cut is minimized.

$$ \text{minimize } c(S,T) = \sum_{u \in S, v \in T} c(u,v) $$

Max-Flow Min-Cut Theorem

The theorem states that the maximum amount of traffic (flow) possible from Source to Sink is equal to the capacity of the minimum bottleneck (cut).

Max Flow = Min Cut = 0
