Minimum Vertex Cover Problem

Problem Statement

A Vertex Cover of a graph \( G = (V, E) \) is a subset of vertices \( V' \subseteq V \) such that for every edge \( (u, v) \in E \), either \( u \in V' \) or \( v \in V' \).

Goal: Find a vertex cover of minimum size.

$$ \text{Minimize } |V'| \text{ such that } \forall (u,v) \in E, (u \in V' \lor v \in V') $$

Computational Complexity

The Minimum Vertex Cover problem is a classic NP-Complete problem. This means for large maps, there is no known fast formula to find the perfect answer, and computers must systematically check combinations!
